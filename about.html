About the Team

Learn more about the University of Utah Club Swim Team

Utah Club Swimming is a co-ed, intercollegiate, competitive, and student-run swim team at the University of Utah. Any currently matriculated student at the University of Utah is eligible to participate, regardless of ability. Practices are held six days a week and are non-mandatory, but highly encouraged. They are hosted by our student officers, who act as both workout leaders and technique coaches.

Competition

As of Fall 2015, our team competes in local meets through US Masters swimming. Competitors at these meets include Utah State Swimming, Boise State Club Swimming, and other area US Masters teams. We compete roughly once per month, with schedules announced at the start of the year. The season concludes with a trip to the East Coast Collegiate Swim & Dive National Championships in Atlanta, Georgia.

History

From humble beginnings

The University of Utah Club Swim Team was founded in the summer of 2014 by Ethan Beseris, who would serve as the team's first President. Joined by Vice President Matt Bird, Men's Team Captain Jeppesen Feliciano, and Women's Team Captain Melissa Hofmann, the "Founding Four" sought to bring the sport of competitive swimming to all students at the University of Utah. They believed that success in the sport is determined not by an individual's talent, but by their willingness to achieve. It is this principle that our team is founded on, that no task should ever be impossible, provided you have the passion and the determination to accomplish it.

How to Join

Become part of something

Anyone with a valid Campus Recreation Services membership is eligible to become a part of our team, including faculty, staff, and alumni. (All current students have an active membership automatically.) Simply print out a tryout form, and then bring the completed form to any team practice you can make it to.
